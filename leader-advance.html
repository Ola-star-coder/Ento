Ashaolu Samuel

Founder, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer

Ashaolu Samuel is the founder, chairman, and CEO of ENTO, a global technology company he launched in 2015. He is responsible for setting the company’s overall vision, innovation strategy, and long-term growth across cybersecurity, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and robotics.

In March 2024, ENTO unveiled CloudNai — an advanced suite of AI-powered tools designed to transform the medical, financial, and legal sectors. Engineered for professionals and institutions, CloudNai offers deeply contextual research capabilities, secure cloud-based operations, and built-in cybersecurity infrastructure at enterprise scale. With the launch, ENTO bridged critical gaps between AI performance and industry-specific compliance, enabling faster diagnosis in healthcare, enhanced fraud detection in finance, and intelligent legal brief generation in seconds. This launch underscored ENTO’s mission to make cutting-edge AI accessible, secure, and deeply integrated into the world’s most sensitive and high-impact industries — further cementing its leadership in responsible innovation and applied intelligence.

Samuel is also the founder of The Ashen Initiative, an independent philanthropic organization launched in 2024 with a mission to shape the ethical frontier of artificial intelligence and expand global opportunity through technology. Rooted in his belief that intelligence — both human and artificial — should serve dignity, equity, and progress, The Ashen Initiative invests in education access, AI ethics research, and tech-driven solutions for climate resilience and digital poverty. With programs spanning Africa, Southeast Asia, and underserved regions in North America, the initiative builds labs, funds youth fellowships, and backs open-source platforms that empower communities to build with AI — not just be shaped by it. Bold in scope and grounded in responsibility, Ashen reflects Samuel’s vision for a future where innovation uplifts everyone, not just the connected few.

Samuel was born in Beverly Hills, California in March 1997. He studied computer science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and later earned a master’s degree in innovation strategy and systems engineering, deepening his multidisciplinary approach to building transformative technologies.
